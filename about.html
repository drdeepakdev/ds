What this site is

Engineering Notes is my public workshop. Every article starts from lived experience — a programme that needed rescuing, an audit finding that taught a lesson, a certification exam sat with real stakes — and ends as the practical, first-person guide I wish had existed at the time. No press-release rewrites, no "top 10 tools" listicles.

I write for working engineers and technology leaders who need to make decisions this quarter, not survey papers. If you read something here and it saves you a week, the site has done its job.

Focus areas

  • Programme & project delivery — PMP, PRINCE2 and agile methods applied to real technology programmes, from business case to benefits.
  • Security, audit & risk — the ISACA disciplines (CISA, CISM, CRISC) plus the attacker's and investigator's mindset from CEH and CHFI.
  • Enterprise architecture — TOGAF-grounded capability thinking, especially applied to AI adoption.
  • Infrastructure roots — Cisco, Microsoft and Oracle platform depth (CCNP, MCSE/MCSA/MCITP/MCTS, OCP) that keeps the strategy honest.
  • Modern engineering — AI agents, cloud-native patterns and platform engineering, written as a leader learning in public.

The road to MVP

I'm working toward the Microsoft MVP award — not for the badge, but because the path there is the point: consistent public writing, speaking, answering questions and contributing to the developer community. Everything I do on that road is tracked openly on the Community page, from articles and talks to forum contributions.
