What this site is

Engineering Notes is my public workshop. Every article here starts as a real problem — an agent that misbehaved, a cloud bill that surprised someone, a platform decision a team had to make — and ends as a practical, code-first write-up of what actually worked. No press-release rewrites, no "top 10 tools" listicles.

I write for working engineers and technology leaders who need to make decisions this quarter, not survey papers. If you read something here and it saves you a week, the site has done its job.

Focus areas

  • AI engineering — production agent systems, the Model Context Protocol, retrieval architectures and the evaluation discipline that keeps them honest.
  • Cloud & Azure — cloud-native .NET, serverless economics, and pragmatic architecture on the Microsoft stack.
  • Platform engineering — internal developer platforms, golden paths and developer-experience metrics.
  • Security for builders — passkeys, zero-trust patterns and supply-chain hygiene for teams without a security department.

The road to MVP

I'm working toward the Microsoft MVP award — not for the badge, but because the path there is the point: consistent public writing, speaking, answering questions and contributing to the developer community. Everything I do on that road is tracked openly on the Community page, from articles and talks to forum contributions.

✍️ Writing weekly 🎤 Speaking & meetups 🧑‍💻 Open source 🎯 MVP candidate track

Beyond the keyboard

When I'm not shipping or writing, I'm usually mentoring engineers earlier in their careers, experimenting with whatever runtime looks interesting this month, or over-engineering something at home that absolutely did not need Kubernetes.

Work with me

I take on a small number of consulting engagements — architecture reviews, AI-adoption roadmaps and platform strategy. If that sounds useful, get in touch or reach me on LinkedIn.
