09 Jul 2026Cybersecurity

Ransomware moved to triple extortion — and the numbers aren't slowing

Data-leak-site listings jumped 213% year over year into 2025, and single-quarter tallies now routinely clear 2,000 named victims. The mechanics changed too: on top of encryption and data theft, crews now add a third lever — DDoS, regulatory complaints filed against the victim, and direct outreach to that victim's own customers and journalists.

Monday move Re-run your tabletop with the triple-extortion script specifically: who talks to regulators, who talks to customers, who talks to press — before the attacker does. That's a CISM/CRISC playbook question, not a firewall one.

07 Jul 2026AI Governance

The EU AI Act's full enforcement lands 2 August 2026

The Act's high-risk obligations activate this August, and the extraterritorial reach means plenty of non-EU organisations are in scope without having noticed. The uncomfortable backdrop: an estimated 82% of enterprises already run AI agents or workflows their security teams didn't know existed. You cannot govern — or attest to a regulator — what you haven't inventoried.

Monday move Stand up an AI system register before you write a single policy. Capability, data touched, owner, risk tier. It's the TOGAF baseline-architecture move applied to AI, and it's the artifact every auditor will ask for first.

Source: 2026, the year of enterprise AI governance ↗
04 Jul 2026Security · Identity

AI identity sprawl is the access-control problem of the year

Across 235 large-enterprise security leaders: 92% lack full visibility into their AI identities, 86% enforce no access policy for them, and 71% report AI systems can reach core business platforms while only 16% actually govern that access. Non-human identities now outnumber humans in most estates, and the newest ones reason and act on their own.

Monday move Treat every agent and service principal as a privileged identity: named owner, scoped credentials, expiry, and an entry in the access review. This is textbook CISA — least privilege and periodic recertification — pointed at a new class of user.

30 Jun 2026Standards

ISO/IEC 42001 is quietly becoming a procurement gate

The world's first AI management-system standard is now held by major providers — AWS, Microsoft, SAP among them — and enterprise procurement teams have started requiring ISO 42001 certification from AI vendors as a condition of purchase. A management-system standard becoming a buying requirement is exactly how ISO 27001 spread through security a decade ago.

Monday move If you sell software with AI in it, start the 42001 gap assessment now — it will show up in your next enterprise RFP. If you buy it, add the certificate to your vendor-risk questionnaire.

Source: AI governance trends 2026 ↗
26 Jun 2026Architecture

Gartner: 40% of enterprise apps will ship task-specific AI agents by year end

Up from under 5% in 2025. That is not a feature trend, it's an architecture event: a new tier of autonomous, non-deterministic components landing inside estates that were never mapped for them. The pragmatic architects are blunt about the failure mode — approach AI without architectural discipline and you get impressive demos and fragile systems.

Monday move Give AI a first-class seat in your architecture: its own capability map, reference pattern, data-lineage view, and non-functional requirements (latency, privacy, explainability, safety). This is the TOGAF answer to a problem everyone is trying to solve with tooling.

Source: The future of EA in the AI era ↗
20 Jun 2026Threat Intel

Agentic AI has climbed to the top of the CISO risk list

Forrester's 2026 read puts autonomous AI agents at the top of enterprise security concerns, alongside near-autonomous nation-state operations and software-supply-chain exposure. Nearly all senior technology leaders — 98% — say they're worried about handing third-party AI systems access to company data. The attack surface is now something that reasons.

Monday move Pull your agent programme into the risk register with a named owner and an accepted-risk sign-off, exactly as you would a new third party. "It's just a productivity tool" is how shadow risk gets funded.
